Virat Kohli

The King | Run Machine | Modern Cricket Legend

Biography

Virat Kohli was born on 5 November 1988 in Delhi, India. He is one of the finest batsmen in modern cricket and known for his aggressive playing style and consistency.

Early Life

Kohli showed interest in cricket from a young age and joined the West Delhi Cricket Academy. His dedication and hard work helped him rise quickly in Indian cricket.

International Career

Virat Kohli made his debut in 2008 and soon became one of India's most reliable batsmen. He captained India across all formats and led the team to many victories.

Major Achievements

  • 🏆 ICC U-19 World Cup Winner (2008 Captain)
  • 🏆 ICC Cricketer of the Year multiple times
  • 🏆 Fastest to many ODI records
  • 🏆 Leading run-scorer in multiple tournaments
  • 🏆 Successful IPL Captain (RCB)

Captaincy & Style

Kohli is known for his aggressive leadership and passion for the game. He brought fitness culture into Indian cricket and always leads from the front.

Career Timeline

📅 2008 – International Debut

📅 2013 – Became ODI Captain

📅 2017 – Full-time Captain in all formats

📅 2018 – ICC Cricketer of the Year

📅 2022 – Stepped down from captaincy

Legacy

Virat Kohli is regarded as one of the greatest batsmen of the modern era. His passion, consistency, and hunger for runs have made him a global cricket icon.

Conclusion

Virat Kohli’s journey reflects determination, discipline, and excellence. His contribution to Indian cricket has been immense, and his legacy continues to inspire future generations.
