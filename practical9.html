01 — Multi-Column Layout

The Art of Reading Flow

The multi-column layout is one of CSS3's most powerful editorial tools, letting text flow naturally across a defined number of columns — just like a newspaper or magazine. Column rules act as elegant separators, guiding the reader's eye from one column to the next without interruption. Responsive breakpoints ensure that on smaller screens the columns gracefully collapse, preserving readability at every viewport width. The column-gap property controls white space between columns, giving designers precise control over visual rhythm and density. Combining column-count with column-width allows CSS to pick the optimal number of columns automatically based on the available space, making the layout fluid and adaptive. Text alignment and hyphenation round out the typographic refinement, ensuring clean ragged edges or perfectly justified blocks of prose depending on the designer's intention. Together these properties bring print-quality typography to the browser.
